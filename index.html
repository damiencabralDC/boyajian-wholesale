How private label works at Boyajian

Five stages from first call to first shipment. Most partners go from agreement to product on shelf in 8 to 14 weeks.

  1. 1

    Discovery

    A 30-minute call. We learn your product, target customer, label requirements, and what success looks like. No NDA needed for first conversations; we'll sign one when we move into recipe specifics.

  2. 2

    Formulation

    Our R&D team builds samples to your spec. Real ingredients only: fresh garlic, real citrus peel, whole herbs. Most projects iterate two or three rounds before the recipe is locked.

  3. 3

    Pilot run

    A small production run to validate the recipe at scale, finalize fill weights and labels, and pull a COA. Most pilots fall in the 200 to 2,000 unit range.

  4. 4

    Production

    A standing schedule with rolling forecast. We hold to your spec across every run, with full lot traceability — from the garlic supplier we've used since 1988 onward.

  5. 5

    Documentation & ship

    Every shipment leaves Canton with the COA, allergen statement, kosher certification, and nutritional panel your buyers expect.

Common questions about private label

There's no universal MOQ. It depends on bottle size, label complexity, and whether the recipe is one we already make. Most first runs land between 1,000 and 5,000 units. We've gone smaller for pilots and much larger for established partners.

You do. We print and apply whatever artwork you provide. We can recommend bottle suppliers, cap suppliers, and label printers we've worked with, but the brand is yours all the way through.

For a recipe we already make in a bottle size we already run: 6 to 8 weeks. For a new formulation or new packaging: 10 to 14 weeks, including formulation rounds and the pilot run.

Our Canton facility runs olive, canola, sunflower, garlic, citrus, herbs, and chili. We're not a peanut, tree nut, dairy, soy, gluten, or shellfish facility. Tell us what allergen statement your buyer needs and we'll confirm fit on the discovery call.

Co-packing means you bring the recipe and we manufacture and bottle. Private label means we build the recipe around your spec, then manufacture and bottle. Many partners start with one and move to the other.
